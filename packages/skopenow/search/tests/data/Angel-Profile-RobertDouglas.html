    What I Do

    I have been working in various areas of marketing for 5 years, and specifically in reporting-based positions for the past 4 years. Most of my focus has been to tie together strategies for individual channels into one cohesive plan. My goal is to continue applying those skills for emerging companies.

    Describe what you have done, what you are doing, and the kinds of things you are interested in.
    Achievements

    One of my greatest “wins” at Jellyfish was critiquing a client’s lead targeting strategy to not only improve their initial goal, but to also change the way we report on the account. Our client wanted to get more paid search leads, while also acquiring them as cost-efficiently as possible. They ran for the low CPL regions, and as result bumped up lead goals there and lowered them in high CPL areas. I countered by creating a paid search state segmentation based on brand knowledge, product choice, and how much each state converted to their priority products.

    This shifted their initial focus of chasing leads in Wyoming and West Virginia because they were available at a low CPL, and instead targeting the most important areas for their success. In the end, 20-24 states provided more than 80% of their traffic and conversions. Now questions focus on how each region and segment is performing and if that is helping or hurting the account. The account itself has flourished ever since this shift.
